EU AI Act Compliance Guide for GP Practices & Healthcare

Healthcare is one of the 8 high-risk categories in Annex III of the EU AI Act. If your GP practice or healthcare organisation uses AI for diagnostics, triage, patient management, or clinical decision support, you face the strictest tier of compliance obligations under Articles 6, 16, and 26.

AI tools commonly used in gp practices & healthcare

AI-powered diagnostic tools, clinical decision support systems, patient triage chatbots, automated appointment scheduling, predictive analytics for patient outcomes, AI-assisted imaging analysis, natural language processing for clinical notes.

Why gp practices & healthcare is classified high-risk

Annex III, Section 5(b) explicitly lists AI systems “intended to be used as safety components in the management and operation of critical digital infrastructure, road traffic, and the supply of water, gas, heating and electricity” — and Section 5(a) covers AI in healthcare: systems “intended to be used for risk assessment and pricing in relation to natural persons in the case of life and health insurance.” Clinical AI falls under the “access to essential services” category.
