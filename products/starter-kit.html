Why do you need this?

The EU AI Act is now law. Article 4 (AI Literacy) has been mandatory since February 2025 — meaning your staff must already be trained. Article 26 lists 12 specific obligations for every business that uses AI systems (called “deployers” in the law). Article 50 requires transparency disclosures to anyone interacting with your AI. And Article 99 sets fines up to €35 million or 7% of global revenue for non-compliance.

This starter kit gives you the documents you need to meet these obligations. Each template maps directly to a specific Article of the regulation.

What’s inside (7 templates)

1. AI System Inventory Template Article 26
Register every AI system: name, provider, purpose, risk category, data processed, human oversight. Article 26(1) requires “appropriate technical and organisational measures.”
2. Risk Classification Flowchart Article 6 + Annex III
Visual decision tree: prohibited, high-risk, limited-risk, or minimal-risk. Based on 8 categories in Annex III.
3. AI Literacy Briefing Template Art. 4 — NOW
Ready-to-deliver staff training briefing. Article 4 requires “sufficient AI literacy” — in force since 2 Feb 2025.
4. Transparency Disclosure Templates Article 50
Notification templates for customers, employees, and visitors interacting with AI systems including chatbots and AI-generated content.
5. Provider Compliance Letter Articles 16 & 25
Formal letter to AI providers requesting compliance documentation. Article 26(5) says you must verify provider compliance.
6. Compliance Checklist Art. 26 (all 12)
All 12 deployer obligations: organisational measures, human oversight, data quality, record-keeping, registration, incident reporting, fundamental rights assessment.
7. Quick Reference Card Summary
One-page summary: key deadlines, risk categories, top actions. Full timeline from Feb 2025 through Aug 2027.

Any EU-based business that uses AI tools. If your organisation uses chatbots, AI-powered analytics, automated decision-making, AI-generated content, or predictive tools — the AI Act applies to you. Specifically built for:

Key compliance deadlines

2 Feb 2025 — Article 4 (AI Literacy): already in force. Non-compliance can be penalised now.

2 Aug 2025 — Articles 5, 111: prohibited AI practices banned, governance structures required.

2 Aug 2026 — Articles 6, 16, 26, 50: high-risk obligations, deployer duties, transparency rules.

2 Aug 2027 — Full enforcement of all remaining provisions including Annex I systems.

Questions

Do I need this if I only use ChatGPT or Copilot?

Yes. If any staff member uses AI tools in their work, your organisation must comply with Article 4 (AI literacy) at minimum. If you use AI to make decisions about people, Articles 26 and 50 also apply.

What format are the templates in?

Fillable PDF. Open in any PDF reader, type directly into the fields, save and share.
