Why do you need this?

The EU AI Act is now law. Article 4 (AI Literacy) has been mandatory since February 2025 — meaning your staff must already be trained. Article 26 lists 12 specific obligations for every business that uses AI systems (called “deployers” in the law). Article 50 requires transparency disclosures to anyone interacting with your AI. And Article 99 sets fines up to €35 million or 7% of global revenue for non-compliance.

This starter kit gives you the documents you need to meet these obligations. Each template maps directly to a specific Article of the regulation.

What’s inside (7 templates)

1. AI System Inventory Template

Article 26

A register of every AI system your organisation uses. Covers system name, provider, purpose, risk category, data processed, and human oversight arrangements. Article 26(1) requires deployers to take “appropriate technical and organisational measures” — this inventory is step one.

2. Risk Classification Flowchart

Article 6 + Annex III

A visual decision tree to classify each AI system as prohibited, high-risk, limited-risk, or minimal-risk. Based on Article 6 criteria and the 8 categories in Annex III. Know exactly which rules apply to each tool you use.

3. AI Literacy Briefing Template

Article 4 — already mandatory

A ready-to-deliver staff training briefing on AI literacy. Article 4 requires “sufficient AI literacy” for all staff operating or affected by AI systems. This obligation has been in force since 2 February 2025 — there is no grace period.

4. Transparency Disclosure Templates

Article 50

Notification templates for customers, employees, and website visitors who interact with your AI systems. Article 50 requires you to inform people when they are interacting with AI — including chatbots, AI-generated content, and emotion recognition systems.

5. Provider Compliance Letter

Articles 16 & 25

A formal letter to send to your AI providers (SaaS vendors, tool suppliers) requesting their compliance documentation. Articles 16 and 25 place obligations on providers — but Article 26(5) says you must verify that documentation exists.

6. Compliance Checklist

Article 26 (all 12 obligations)

A tick-by-tick checklist covering all 12 deployer obligations under Article 26: organisational measures, human oversight, data quality monitoring, record-keeping, registration, incident reporting, fundamental rights impact assessments, and more.

7. Quick Reference Card

Summary

A one-page summary of key deadlines, risk categories, and your top compliance actions. Pin it to the wall or share with your team. Covers the full timeline from February 2025 (AI literacy) through August 2027 (full enforcement).

Key compliance deadlines

2 Feb 2025 — Article 4 (AI Literacy): already in force. Non-compliance can be penalised now.

2 Aug 2025 — Articles 5, 111: prohibited AI practices banned, governance structures required.

2 Aug 2026 — Articles 6, 16, 26, 50: high-risk obligations, deployer duties, transparency rules take full effect.

2 Aug 2027 — Full enforcement of all remaining provisions including Annex I systems.

Questions

Do I need this if I only use ChatGPT or Copilot?

Yes. If any staff member uses AI tools in their work, your organisation must comply with Article 4 (AI literacy) at minimum. If you use AI to make decisions about people, Articles 26 and 50 also apply.
